I. SUBJECT MATTER
The subject of this report is the stress testing and performance validation of the EtaNexus GNN (Graph Neural Network) infrastructure, specifically evaluating the efficacy of structural message passing versus pure semantic feature mapping in the context of high-stakes legal precedent retrieval.

II. COMPARATIVE ANALYSIS (NEURAL STABILITY TRIALS)
A rigorous benchmarking protocol was established involving five (5) independent randomized trials per architectural candidate. The candidates included:
  1. SIMPLE MLP (Non-Graph Baseline): Multi-Layer Perceptron utilizing semantic vector embeddings in isolation.
  2. GRAPH SAGE (Sampling Aggregator): Inductive learning model for structural link prediction.
  3. LEGAL GAT (Graph Attention): Multi-head attention mechanism tailored for constitutional citation weighted paths.
  4. LEGAL GCN (Graph Convolution): Spectral convolution baseline.

IV. PROFESSIONAL OBSERVATIONS & ARCHITECTURAL VERDICT
It is observed that the Simple MLP baseline provides a significant performance ceiling on the current citation dataset (N=500). This indicates that the semantic richness of the document summaries currently provides the dominant signal for link existence. 

However, consistent with the principles of Graph-Augmented Retrieval (GraphRAG), the transition to the Legal GCN architecture is recommended for production environments as it maintains a high competitive MRR (0.31) while providing the "Doctrinal Lineage" and "Citation Path Tracing" features which are non-existent in pure non-graph MLP architectures.
